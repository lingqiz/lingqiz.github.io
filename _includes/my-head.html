{% comment %}{% endcomment %} {% comment %} Sticky, always-centered footer. The theme positions the footer with `position: absolute`. Its containing block changes during navigation: push-state.js applies a `transform` to #_main for the page-fade animation, and a transformed element becomes the containing block for its absolutely-positioned descendants. That made the copyright reflow and slide from the right to the center on load — very visible on narrow screens, where it briefly wrapped to a narrow column on the right. Instead we lay #_main out as a flex column and let the footer sit in normal flow, pushed to the bottom with margin-top:auto, so a transform on #_main just moves it uniformly (no reflow) and it stays centered from the first paint. Using flex (not position:relative on #_main) avoids turning #_main into a containing block for other absolute/fixed descendants. {% endcomment %}
